Our Design Choices

User-Centered Design

- We utilized several principles from the UCD model

- Our design is based specifically on meeting users' needs

- We focused on allowing users to complete any relevant use cases

- Students should be able to easily access any relevant resources, as this is the main functional requirement of the website

Navigation

- We focused on keeping our navigation structure simple

- A complicated navigation structure would likely distract from the important content of the website

- There are three main pages: Projects, Advisors, and About

- All pages can be accessed easily via the navbar

Structure/Spacing

- We focused on keeping our website structure open, with a good amount of margins and blank space

- Our sections where projects and advisors are displayed take up the majority of the page

- Students should be able to very clearly see what their search results have returned, and should be able to filter through these results themselves

- Any "junk" content that isn't related to specific use cases from students was placed in the margins of the page

Color Palette

- Our color palette features red, white, and black

- We chose to use the same colors as the SCU website; we got the hex values directly from scu.edu's stylesheet

- SCU students will likely be comfortable with this color palette

Banner/Title

- We included a small banner at the top of the page

- This was done mainly for aesthetics, and to match the SCU-type design

- All of our titles/banners are very minimal; we don't want huge banners taking up a lot of valuable space

- We instead focused on creating banners with contrasting colors to allow them to stand out, but still be out of the way

Accessibility

- Although our website is designed for students, accessibility is still extremely important to consider

- We utilized large font for important content

- Additionally, our color palette provides high-contrast colors which should benefit visually impaired users.

- Our simple design naturally makes it easier for people with cognitive disabilities to navigate the website and utilize all of its functions
